FINDINGS — Kitchen NFE / sampling-variance sweep
==============================================================================
Runs: 31  |  Plots: 01–07 in this directory

CLAIM #1 — Quality at equal/low NFE: SUPPORTED
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
At NFE ∈ {1,2,4}, FlowPolicy completes ~3.4–3.8 tasks/episode while
both DP models collapse to ~0.1–0.3 tasks (p3=0).

DP only recovers when NFE ≥ ~50:
  DP-CNN: @1=0.28  @32=0.04  @50=3.68  @100=3.98
  DP-Transformer: @1=0.22  @32=0.52  @50=3.90  @100=3.92
  FlowPolicy: @1=3.38  @2=3.78  @4=3.66

Latency note: FP@1 ≈ 14ms vs DP@100 ≈ 586–638ms (~40–45×).
At equal low NFE, DP is fast but useless; FP is both fast and competent.

CLAIM #2 — Lower sampling variance from ODE: MIXED / NOT CLEARLY SUPPORTED
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  DP-CNN           NFE=100  mean_tasks=3.992±0.030  (n=5)
  DP-Transformer   NFE=100  mean_tasks=3.968±0.039  (n=5)
  FlowPolicy       NFE=1    mean_tasks=3.420±0.051  (n=5)

FP mean_tasks std (0.051) is slightly HIGHER than DP-CNN (0.030) and
DP-Transformer (0.039). Per-task SR std is similar (~0.048–0.058).
So ODE vs ancestral-SDE does not produce a clear reproducibility win
for FlowPolicy in this Kitchen setup (5 seeds × 50 episodes).
Likely reason: both policies still sample random initial noise each
predict_action; env init is fixed, but policy stochasticity from a0
dominates outcome variance more than per-step DDPM noise here.

BOTTOM LINE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Yes — FlowPolicy is far better at low NFE (the big quality claim).
2. No clear win — deterministic ODE does not yield tighter eval std
   than DDPM across sampling seeds in this experiment.
3. At full DP budget (NFE=100), DP still edges FP on mean tasks
   (~3.97–3.99 vs FP@1 ~3.42), matching the original Kitchen eval.
