BASELINE REPORT — Why existing Kitchen eval cannot verify the two claims
==============================================================================

Sources: summary.json from the 100-ep × 3-checkpoint Kitchen eval.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1) Claim #1 (success vs NFE) — NOT TESTED
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Existing eval uses a single NFE point per model:
  FlowPolicy:  num_inference_step = 1  (CCFM, sigma_var=0)
  DP (CNN/TF): num_inference_steps = 100 (DDPM ancestral)
Comparing FP@1 vs DP@100 mixes quality with compute budget.
A fair quality-at-equal-NFE claim needs a sweep (planned separately).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4) Sampling mechanics (why claim #2 is plausible a priori)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FlowPolicy: sigma_var=0 → sigma_t=0 → no per-step noise; only initial
  torch.randn. ODE-like given initial noise.
DP DDPM: scheduler.step injects noise each step (ancestral/SDE).
Existing eval does not fix sampling_seed, so reproducibility unmeasured.
