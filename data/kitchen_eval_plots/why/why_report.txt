  DP-Transformer num_tasks_completed: 2:0%, 3:4%, 4:96%, 5:0%
    last task: Top=1%, Light=11%, Slide=40%, Hinge=48%

FP mass at 3–4 tasks, last task often Slide. DP mass at 4–5 tasks, last
task often Hinge — hence higher p3/p4 and hinge/light/top success.

5) Why FlowPolicy is faster
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  FlowPolicy       latency=   14.3 ms  n_ctrl=  94.0  n_ctrl×lat=    1340 ms  episode_dur=   23500 ms
  DP-CNN           latency=  634.8 ms  n_ctrl=  35.0  n_ctrl×lat=   22218 ms  episode_dur=   23333 ms
  DP-Transformer   latency=  564.9 ms  n_ctrl=  35.0  n_ctrl×lat=   19772 ms  episode_dur=   23333 ms

FP: many cheap predict calls (~94 × ~14 ms).
DP: few expensive predict calls (~35 × ~600 ms).
Wall-clock episode duration tracks the expensive DP inference, not step count.

6) Direct answers
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Why is FlowPolicy better at kettle & bottom burner?
A: Not because they are first. FP more often commits to the early chain
   MW→Kettle→Bottom (74% MW→Kettle vs ~48–50% for DP) and converts kettle
   completions into bottom (78%). DP branches away from that early chain
   more often (MW→Bottom or Kettle→Light), so fewer kettle/bottom successes.

Q: Why is Diffusion Policy better at hinge, light switch, top burner?
A: After bottom/top, DP continues into Top→Light→Hinge much more often.
   FP after bottom prefers Slide and then almost always STOPS — it rarely
   attempts the mid/late cabinet/switch chain. Same start position, different
   next-task policy.

Q: Why is FlowPolicy faster?
A: Per-call inference is ~40–45× cheaper. Even with more control steps,
   total inference budget and episode wall-clock stay much lower.

Q: Why is Diffusion Policy better at multi-stage?
A: DP finishes 4–5 tasks in nearly every episode and often ends on hinge.
   FP frequently ends the chain at slide after 3–4 tasks, so p3/p4 drop.
